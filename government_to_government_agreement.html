Training for G2G Agreement for Caregivers in Israel

The Government-to-Government Agreement for the recruitment of caregivers in Israel is designed to streamline the process of sending trained caregivers from various countries to Israel for employment in the caregiving sector. This agreement ensures that caregivers are adequately trained, compliant with regulations, and prepared to provide high-quality care to elderly and disabled individuals in Israel.

About the Agreement

  • The Government-to-Government Agreement facilitates the legal and regulated recruitment of caregivers to work in Israel’s thriving healthcare sector, specifically in the field of elderly and disability care. This bilateral agreement ensures that caregivers undergo training that meets Israeli standards, ensuring quality care for Israeli citizens.

Purpose

  • To provide Israel with skilled and qualified caregivers.
  • To offer employment opportunities to trained caregivers in Israel.
  • To ensure caregivers' legal rights and proper working conditions in Israel.

Training Program Overview

The training program for caregivers under the Government-to-Government Agreement is designed to equip individuals with the necessary skills, knowledge, and qualifications to deliver quality care to elderly and disabled individuals in Israeli homes and institutions. The training program focuses on practical care, cultural sensitivity, language skills, and the legal and ethical responsibilities of caregivers in Israel.

Key Components of the Training
  • Caregiving Skills
    • Basic and advanced patient care
    • Mobility assistance and support
    • Medication administration
    • Infection control practices
  • Emergency Procedures
    • First Aid and CPR
    • Managing medical emergencies
    • Dealing with elderly patients with dementia or disability
  • Cultural Awareness and Communication
    • Understanding Israeli culture and the specific needs of elderly individuals in Israel
    • Basic Hebrew language skills for better communication
    • Patient-centered care and emotional support
  • Legal and Ethical Aspects
    • Rights and responsibilities of caregivers under Israeli law
    • Workplace safety and labor regulations
    • Confidentiality and ethical conduct
  • Certification
    • Upon successful completion of the training program, caregivers will receive certification that qualifies them for employment under the Government-to- Government Agreement.

Program Duration & Structure

  • Training Duration: The program typically lasts 25 days, with 50 hours of online sessions.
  • Schedule: Sessions are held daily, with flexible timings to accommodate applicants.

Training Process and Timeline

  • Selection of Candidates
    • Caregivers are selected based on specific criteria outlined by both governments.
    • Applicants must meet the necessary health, educational, and experience requirements.
  • Pre-Departure Training
    • Duration: 25 days
    • Caregivers undergo comprehensive theoretical training in India.
    • Language classes (Hebrew) and cultural orientation are included.
  • Certification and Accreditation
    • Candidates must pass interview to obtain certification.
  • Placement in Israel
    • After passing the interview, caregivers are placed with employers in Israel, with assistance from placement agencies authorized under the agreement.

Key Components of the Training

For Caregivers

  • Access to reliable, well-paying jobs in Israel
  • Gain internationally recognized caregiving skills
  • Training fully aligned with Israeli healthcare requirements
  • Legal and contractual job placement in Israel
  • Support and guidance from both governments throughout the process

Post-Training Placement

  • Guaranteed Job Placement: Caregivers who successfully passed the interview will be placed with employers in Israel under the government-to- government agreement.
  • Support Services:
    • Pre-departure orientation
    • Job placement assistance
    • Ongoing support during employment in Israel

FAQs

What is the duration of the training program?

The training typically lasts 25 days, depending on the specific requirements.

Do I need to speak Hebrew before the training?

Basic language skills will be taught as part of the training. Prior knowledge is not required.

Will I receive a job offer after training?

Yes, upon successful passing of interview, caregivers will be placed with employers in Israel.
